The Problem with Big Box Stores

For too long, hardworking farmers have been squeezed out by corporate middlemen, while families pay more for eggs that have lost their farm-fresh quality.

Store

Corporate Middleman Markup

Big box stores add 200-400% markup, making farm-fresh eggs unaffordable while farmers receive pennies on the dollar.

Warehouse

Warehouse Storage Delays

Eggs sit in distribution centers for weeks, losing their farm-fresh taste and nutritional value before reaching your table.

Unknown

No Connection to Your Farmer

You have no idea which farm your eggs came from, how the chickens were raised, or when they were actually laid.

Factory

Limited to Factory Farm Eggs

Grocery stores only carry mass-produced eggs, leaving heritage breeds and specialty varieties unavailable to most families.

Struggle

Farmers Struggle to Survive

Small family farmers can't compete with industrial operations and struggle to reach customers willing to pay fair prices.

Platform

No Modern Direct-to-Consumer Platform

The egg industry lacks a user-friendly way for families to discover and buy directly from local farmers in their community.
